Section 01

Project Overview

A 200-person professional services company was processing new hire onboarding through a mix of emailed PDF attachments, manual HR data entry, and a two-week lag between start date and full system access. With 30–40 new hires per quarter, HR staff was spending the equivalent of a full-time role on onboarding paperwork alone.

We built an end-to-end onboarding portal that automates document collection, routes forms for signature and approval, syncs data into their HRIS, and triggers account provisioning — compressing their 14-day onboarding timeline to a single business day.

Section 02

The Challenge

New employees were arriving on their first day without system credentials, equipment assignments not confirmed, and paperwork still pending — creating a poor first impression and real productivity loss. HR was manually chasing signatures, re-keying form data into multiple systems, and tracking completion status in a spreadsheet.

  • No centralized tracking — HR had no single view of which new hires had completed which steps
  • Manual data re-entry into three separate systems (HRIS, payroll, IT provisioning) for every new hire
  • No automated reminders — incomplete forms were discovered on start date, not before
  • IT access requests submitted by email, processed manually, averaging 5–7 business days to complete
Section 03

The Solution

We designed a pre-boarding portal that new hires access before their start date, walking them through document submission, e-signature, and personal information collection — all feeding automatically into downstream systems.

  • Offer-Triggered Invitation: When HR marks an offer as accepted, the portal sends the new hire a personalized onboarding link with their unique pre-boarding checklist
  • Document Collection & E-Signature: I-9, W-4, direct deposit, policy acknowledgements — all collected digitally with legally compliant e-signature capture and automatic archival
  • Automated Reminder Sequences: Incomplete items trigger daily reminder emails until resolved; escalation alerts sent to HR manager if outstanding 3 days before start date
  • HRIS Data Sync: Completed form data is parsed and pushed directly to the HRIS via API — zero manual re-entry for HR
  • IT Provisioning Trigger: Upon document completion, an automated provisioning request is sent to IT with role-based system access requirements — accounts created and credentials emailed before day one
  • Manager Dashboard: Hiring managers see real-time completion status for their incoming team members and receive a day-one readiness report 48 hours before each start date

Section 05

Key Results

Time-to-full-access compressed from 14 business days to 1 — new hires now have credentials, equipment assignment, and system access confirmed before their start date.

  • HR onboarding labor reduced by 78% — from approximately 6 hours per hire to 1.3 hours
  • 100% document completion rate achieved — automated reminders and pre-start escalation eliminated day-one paperwork surprises
  • Data re-entry errors reduced to zero — all form data flows directly into HRIS without human transcription
  • New hire satisfaction scores on first-week surveys improved by 34 points (NPS scale) after launch
  • System processed 140 new hires in first year with no manual interventions required
